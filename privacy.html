Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

SaadOs ("the app") is a personal daily companion for prayer, intention, journaling, and reflection. This page explains what data the app accesses, how it's stored, and what it does (and doesn't) do with it.

What data SaadOs stores

SaadOs stores the content you create in the app — your daily entries, prayer tracking, journal text, photos, videos, budgets, and preferences. This data is stored in two places:

  • Locally on your device, in your browser's storage, so the app works instantly.
  • In your own Google Drive (if you choose to connect it), inside a folder named "SaadOs" that the app creates for you.
SaadOs has no server or database of its own. The app runs entirely in your browser. Nobody else — including the developer — can access, view, or collect your journal entries, photos, or any other content you create. Your data belongs to you and stays in your Google Drive account.

Google Drive access

If you choose to connect Google Drive, SaadOs requests permission using Google's "drive.file" scope. This is a limited permission that only allows the app to:

  • Create a folder called "SaadOs" in your Drive
  • Read, write, and delete files inside that folder — the journal entries, photos, and videos you add through the app

SaadOs cannot see, access, or modify any other files, folders, or photos in your Google Drive. It has no access to your Gmail, Google Photos, Calendar, or any other Google service.

What SaadOs does NOT do

  • It does not run ads or ad tracking of any kind.
  • It does not sell, share, or transmit your data to any third party.
  • It does not use analytics services that track your personal activity.
  • It does not have access to your data — your entries are stored only on your device and/or your own Google Drive.

Deleting your data

You can delete all app data at any time:

  • Clear your browser's site data for SaadOs to remove locally stored data.
  • Delete the "SaadOs" folder from your Google Drive to remove all synced content.
  • Revoke SaadOs's access at any time via your Google Account permissions page.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date at the top of this page will be revised. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.
